CareChanger

A low-cost, void event detection and monitoring technology.

A practical care solution for medical personnel.

The CareChanger platform is a wheelchair embedded sensor for detecting void events among incontinent wheelchair users, coupled with a communication and application service for care providers.

Real-time data collection for optimal patient care.

Temperature and humidity sensors embedded in the device collect real time data. With an algorithm optimized to detect and notify a caretaker of a void event within a few minutes of occurrence, the patient’s quality of care is greatly improved.

Easy Wi-Fi connectivity.

Once a void event has been detected, a message is sent through a Wi-Fi connection to notify a caretaker of the event, making it easier to provide adequate care.

Live, automatically updated trend graphs.

With real-time data collection, a graph of all past patient void events are immediately generated on the web app, allowing caretakers to track patient trends and activities.

Large scalability.

With the option to manage various patients and care groups, CareChanger can be implemented in nursing homes. Caretakers can have different care groups with different patients, and they even have the flexibility to care for patients in different care locations.

Jim and Muriel

Jim, CareChanger’s President, was driven to create the CareChanger device for his daughter Muriel, who is urinarily incontinent. After suffering from urinary tract infections and skin breakdowns from her wet diaper, Jim decided to build a sensing device using a wireless sensor tag. UVic BMED has been developing and refining a working prototype of the device, along with the accompanying web application.
